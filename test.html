Introduction

Lassa fever (LF), a viral hemorrhagic illness endemic in West Africa, causes an estimated 5000 fatalities annually. LF vaccines are currently in development and not yet licensed for use. Factors that influence prioritization of a future licensed vaccine by healthcare policy decision-makers in affected countries, in the context of competing priorities, are currently unclear. A scoping literature review and Key Informant interviews were conducted to provide data on such factors.

Objectives

  • Determine factors influencing decision-making by policymakers on prioritization of Lassa Fever vaccines procurement in affected countries.
  • Identify perceived barriers to the prioritization of Lassa Fever vaccines procurement.
  • Analyze Lassa Fever vaccine characteristics considered enablers for the prioritization of their procurement.

Methods

Scoping Literature Review

📚
Literature Search

Literature search of bibliographic databases, guided by the PRISMA Scoping Review Extension recommendations

🗓️
Target duration

10-year period from 2014 to 2024 during which Lassa fever vaccine development has accelerated.

🔍
Search Criteria

Restricted to articles published in the English language.

Search terms used: “Lassa Fever vaccine introduction”, "Lassa Fever vaccine adoption”, “Lassa fever vaccine prioritization” and“Lassa Fever vaccination policy”.

Key Informant Interviews (KIIs)

👥
Interview Structure

Semi-structured interviews of officials from government agencies and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) that influence healthcare policy decision-making

📝
Questionnaire

Open-ended questions on healthcare policy decision-making

📊
Data Analysis

A thematic analysis was conducted, using the Burchett framework for new vaccine introduction as a guide (Burchett HED et al., 2012).

Key Findings

Number of published articles by year in bibliographic databases

Conclusions and Recommendations

  • Limited evidence from published literature to inform decision-making for prioritization of Lassa Fever vaccine procurement in affected countries.
  • Further research needed to validate perceived barriers and key enabling factors for Lassa Fever vaccine procurement and prioritization.
  • Major consideration: vaccine safety, high efficacy, and long-lasting immunity, given the high case fatality rate of Lassa Fever.
  • Financial support needed to facilitate the adoption of Lassa Fever vaccines due to affordability concerns.
