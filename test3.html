Background

Prior to 2016, Afghanistan lacked specialized cancer programs. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) relied on neighboring countries' data for estimates. The 2016 launch of the Cancer Diagnosis and Treatment Project, evolving into the National Cancer Control Program (NCCP) in 2017, marked significant progress. The NCCP established the Kabul Cancer Registry (KCR), enabling Afghanistan's first domestic cancer epidemiology study.

Objective

Report the first cancer incidence in Afghanistan using population-based cancer registry data, analyze data collected from over 50 health facilities in Kabul between 2018 and 2020, and provide a foundation for future cancer research and policy in Afghanistan.

Methods

Data Collection

Gathered from 50+ Kabul health facilities (2018-2020)

Analysis Tool

CanReg-5 software for data storage and assessment

Incidence Calculation

Crude, age-specific, and age-standardized rates per 100,000

Results

Lessons Learned

  1. First cancer estimates for Afghanistan based on population-based registry data.
  2. IARC should consider these data for future cancer incidence estimates in Afghanistan.
  3. Findings provide crucial baseline for national cancer control planning and resource allocation.
  4. Continued support and expansion of cancer registry efforts are recommended.
